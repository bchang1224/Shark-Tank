The Origin

Why We Started Brandon's Backyard Botanica

Driving through Ventura neighborhoods, our founder Brandon Chang kept seeing the same thing: homeowners spending thousands of dollars a year watering lawns and exotic shrubs that had no business being in a semi-arid coastal California climate. The sprinklers running at noon. The brown patches every August. The bills.

It didn't make sense. California has one of the richest native floras on Earth — thousands of beautiful, low-water plants that evolved here over millions of years and ask for almost nothing in return. Yet most nurseries still shelve the same imported ornamentals that require constant watering, fertilizing, and pesticide applications just to survive here.

Brandon's Backyard Botanica was founded to fix that disconnect: to make California native plants accessible, affordable, and approachable for every homeowner in our region.

🌊 Water Stewardship

A native garden uses 85% less water per year than traditional turf landscaping. That's real money saved every month — and a direct contribution to California's water security.

🦋 Ecological Integrity

CA native plants support 1,600+ native bee species — roughly 44% of all bee species in North America. Exotic ornamentals simply cannot do this.

🏡 Homeowner Value

Native plants require 80% less water to maintain than non-native species. Lower bills, lower maintenance, and a yard that looks better with less effort.

🔬 Evidence-Based

Over 5,500 plant species are native to California — 40% found nowhere else on Earth (UC Santa Cruz). We protect and propagate this irreplaceable biodiversity.

Founded in Ventura, 2026

We operate a nursery in Ventura, California — growing many of our plants on-site from local seed stock to preserve regional genetic diversity. Every plant we sell is proven for Ventura County's coastal climate, soils, and rainfall patterns.

We serve homeowners across Ventura County and into Santa Barbara and LA Counties, offering delivery, installation, and ongoing care.

Brandon Chang

Co-Founder & Head Horticulturist

Brandon grew up in Ventura County, spending his summers hiking the Santa Monica Mountains and his afternoons in his grandfather's garden. He watched the landscape he loved dry out, fragment, and get replaced by thirsty ornamentals that had no ecological connection to the place.

After earning a degree in Plant Science from Cal Poly San Luis Obispo and spending several years with the California Native Plant Society's Ventura chapter, Brandon co-founded Backyard Botanica in 2026 with a clear vision: make native plants the default, not the exception, for Ventura County homeowners.

He knows every plant in the nursery by its Latin name and can identify a native bee species on sight. When he's not building gardens, he's reseeding riparian corridors in the Santa Clara River watershed.

🦋

Lucio Vilela

Landscape Designer

Lucio brings a background in ecological restoration and landscape architecture to every project. Originally from Ojai, he trained at the UC Santa Barbara Botanic Garden before joining the Botanica team. His designs are known for integrating visual drama with rigorous ecological function. He has a particular passion for Mediterranean-climate plant communities and spends his weekends photographing native bees in Ventura County's coastal scrub.

Water Conservation Specialist

If there's a rebate dollar available, Liam will find it.

Gibson MacDougall

Gibson co-founded Backyard Botanica alongside Brandon and oversees all plant propagation and nursery operations, ensuring our stock is healthy, ethically sourced, and genetically appropriate for Ventura County's climate. He also coaches youth soccer on weekends.
